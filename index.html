What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay to the lender every month until the loan is fully repaid. It includes both principal repayment and interest.

Formula: EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ - 1)

Where P = Principal, r = Monthly Interest Rate, n = Number of Months

Types of Loans

  • Home Loan: 6-9% p.a., 10-30 years tenure
  • Car Loan: 7-12% p.a., 1-7 years tenure
  • Personal Loan: 10-24% p.a., 1-5 years
  • Education Loan: 8-15% p.a., up to 15 years
  • Business Loan: 10-20% p.a., 1-10 years

How to Reduce Your EMI

  • Make a larger down payment to reduce principal
  • Negotiate a lower interest rate with good credit
  • Choose a longer repayment tenure
  • Make partial prepayments when possible
  • Transfer your loan to a lender with lower rates
